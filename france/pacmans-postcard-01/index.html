Inside the Machine · Rule Finder, Word Predictor, Storyteller.
▸
What we ran A first hands-on encounter with generative AI. Three short activities, each played twice: once by the youth themselves, once by a generative AI solution. A guessing game with seventy-two image cards to feel out pattern recognition. A word-by-word prediction game played by hand to show how AI text actually works. A short collaborative story to see how the AI reuses context. The point was to compare, side by side, how humans and machines tackle the same task.

Reinforcement Learning · unplugged to plugged.
▸
What we ran Three hours to figure out how a machine actually learns. First half unplugged: a six-by-six coloured grid laid on the floor became a maze with hidden traps and a hidden goal. The group played the learning agent, one square at a time, earning reward tokens when they moved well and losing the lot when they hit a trap. Each youth filled a blank sheet to invent their own way of mapping safe and trapped squares. Second half on real robots (Sphero Indi, Vinci Bot, Tale Bot), programmed through navigation and creative missions, with the objective of reproducing the safest route to the hidden goal.

Giving them a blank sheet for memorising their learning model is what lets those personal strategies appear, and then feed the collective discussion.

The Stupid Invention, part 1 · ideation.
▸
What we ran The session opened with a quiz: a slideshow of unusual products, real or AI-generated. The youth guessed which was real or faked and discussed what gave each one away. They then paired up to design a deliberately useless product, filling a short ideation sheet with the problem, the product idea, a name, a quick explanation and a sketch. The AI was on hand as a co-creator, especially to push the idea further or expand the description.

Coupe de France de Robotique · full day immersion.
▸
What we ran A full day at the Coupe de France de Robotique, the French national robotics competition held in La Roche-sur-Yon. The youth were able to discover robotics projects built by young makers, and to watch the matches, especially the junior finale. They also participated in two hands-on workshops with the association Planète Sciences Vendée: building a small "insect" robot from wire, a battery and a motor, then designing a keychain with a 3D pen.
