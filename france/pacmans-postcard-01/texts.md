## Fiche 01 · Dec 2025 · accent pink

**Title:** Inside the Machine · Rule Finder, Word Predictor, Storyteller.

**What we ran:**
A first hands-on encounter with generative AI. Three short activities, each played twice: once by the youth themselves, once by a generative AI solution. A guessing game with seventy-two image cards to feel out pattern recognition. A word-by-word prediction game played by hand to show how AI text actually works. A short collaborative story to see how the AI reuses context. The point was to compare, side by side, how humans and machines tackle the same task.

**Activity:**
- AI Lab, Inside the Machine
- Rule Finder, Word Predictor, Storyteller · 2h30

**Who:**
- 6 researchers
- 12 to 15 yo

**Mood:**
Curious and engaging. Lots of laughter when they caught the AI making things up.

**Skill tree:** pattern recognition · prompting · critical thinking · data literacy

**Best practice:**
At twelve to fifteen, the youth engage much faster when they play the activity first and meet the AI after. Doing the task themselves gives them something to compare against, and the AI stops feeling like magic.

## Fiche 02 · Jan 2026 · accent blue

**Title:** Reinforcement Learning · unplugged to plugged.

**What we ran:**
Three hours to figure out how a machine actually learns. First half unplugged: a six-by-six coloured grid laid on the floor became a maze with hidden traps and a hidden goal. The group played the learning agent, one square at a time, earning reward tokens when they moved well and losing the lot when they hit a trap. Each youth filled a blank sheet to invent their own way of mapping safe and trapped squares. Second half on real robots (Sphero Indi, Vinci Bot, Tale Bot), programmed through navigation and creative missions, with the objective of reproducing the safest route to the hidden goal.

**Activity:**
- Reinforce Your Learning
- Bio-inspired, unplugged-to-plugged · 3h

**Who:**
- 10 in the lab
- 12 to 15 yo

**Mood:**
Loud and hands-on. The creative missions inspired and engaged youth. The robots turned into a quiet competition between the groups.

**Skill tree:** reinforcement learning · robotics · bio-inspired · debugging

**Best practice:**
At this age, each youth invents their own way of mapping the maze: some go systematic from the start, others lean on memory, some draw pictures. Giving them a blank sheet for memorising their learning model is what lets those personal strategies appear, and then feed the collective discussion.

## Fiche 03 · Mar 2026 · accent orange

**Title:** The Stupid Invention, part 1 · ideation.

**What we ran:**
The session opened with a quiz: a slideshow of unusual products, real or AI-generated. The youth guessed which was real or faked and discussed what gave each one away. They then paired up to design a deliberately useless product, filling a short ideation sheet with the problem, the product idea, a name, a quick explanation and a sketch. The AI was on hand as a co-creator, especially to push the idea further or expand the description.

**Activity:**
- The Stupid Invention, part one
- Creating the product with AI co-pilot · 2h30

**Who:**
- 6 inventors
- 12 to 15 yo

**Mood:**
Playful and focused. The absurd brief lifted the pressure to be right, and they kept iterating until their idea made them laugh.

**Skill tree:** prompting · creativity · hallucination spotting

**Best practice:**
A deliberately silly brief takes the pressure to be right off the youth. At twelve to fifteen, they feel free to experiment, push the AI further, and end up running many more iterations than they would on a serious topic.

## Fiche 04 · Apr 2026 · accent yellow

**Title:** The Stupid Invention, part 2 · building the fake.

**What we ran:**
The session opened with the facilitator showing a fake but very convincing-looking product: real or AI? The brief for the rest of the day was clear: take the absurd invention from the previous session and build everything you would need to sell it, from text and visuals to a physical prototype, credible enough that an outside reader could be fooled.

**Activity:**
- The Stupid Invention, part two
- Convincing investors · 2h30

**Who:**
- 8 pitchers
- 12 to 15 yo

**Mood:**
Concentrated, a bit conspiratorial. They got into the persuasion game and helped each other sharpen the fake.

**Skill tree:** fact-checking · argumentation · prompt iteration

**Best practice:**
Asking the youth to build the fake themselves, rather than just spot one, is what makes the lesson stick. At this age they notice the manipulation techniques much better when they have to use them.

## Fiche 05 · Apr 2026 · accent green

**Title:** The Stupid Invention, part 3 · final pitch.

**What we ran:**
The session opened with the youth themselves recapping the project to each other. Roles were then split: one focused on the cardboard prototype, the others on the investor slideshow. The evening ended with a full pitch in front of a jury, and a Q&A where the youth had to make clear which parts of their pitch were their own work and which came from the AI.

**Activity:**
- The Stupid Invention, part three
- The public pitch · 3h

**Who:**
- 4 finalists
- 12 to 15 yo

**Mood:**
Nervous before, proud after. The Q&A turned into a defence of their own choices.

**Skill tree:** public speaking · authorship · AI awareness

**Best practice:**
Letting the returning youth recap the project in their own words gives them ownership of the work and surfaces what really stuck for them from the previous session. New youth joining mid-sequence settle in much faster when their peers explain the project than when a facilitator does.

## Fiche 06 · May 2026 · accent pink

**Title:** Coupe de France de Robotique · full day immersion.

**What we ran:**
A full day at the Coupe de France de Robotique, the French national robotics competition held in La Roche-sur-Yon. The youth were able to discover robotics projects built by young makers, and to watch the matches, especially the junior finale. They also participated in two hands-on workshops with the association Planète Sciences Vendée: building a small “insect” robot from wire, a battery and a motor, then designing a keychain with a 3D pen.

**Activity:**
- French Robotics Cup, finale
- Off-site immersion · 8h

**Who:**
- 6 on the road
- 12 to 15 yo

**Mood:**
Wide-eyed. They came back fired up, already talking about coming next year as competitors rather than spectators.

**Skill tree:** systems thinking · field observation · failure analysis

**Best practice:**
Seeing competitors their own age, sometimes younger, changed what the youth thought they could do themselves. They can picture themselves in a year from now, participating in the next round of the competition with their own robot.
